# Longlist: Leeds Secondary School

**Project**: Build a 1,200-place secondary school in inner-east Leeds to absorb expected pupil growth from new housing.
**Counterfactual**: No build. Existing schools absorb additional pupils via temporary classrooms; capacity reaches breach by 2030 with rising travel times to alternative schools.
**Framework**: uk-gb
**Sponsor**: government
**Stage**: obc
**Price base**: 2024
**Horizon**: 60 years
**Construction**: 3 years
**Ramp-up**: 3 years to full enrolment

## Options

| # | Option | Description |
|---|--------|------------|
| 1 | Do Nothing | Continued reliance on existing schools, temporary classrooms, longer commutes |
| 2 | Do Minimum | 600-place expansion of two existing schools |
| 3 | Preferred | New 1,200-place secondary school on identified site |

## Benefits

| Name | Description | Materiality | Cash flow | Quantification | Monetisation |
|------|-------------|-------------|-----------|----------------|--------------|
| Reduced school travel time | Pupils avoid longer commutes from 2028 | H | non_cash | Person-hours saved per year | DfT TAG VTTS, GBP 7.50/hr |
| Educational attainment uplift | Smaller classes, modern facilities | H | non_cash | Pupil-years of improved attainment | WELLBY at GBP 13k per WELLBY |
| Avoided temporary classroom maintenance | Counterfactual cost avoided | M | cash_in | Avoided LA spending | LA cost data, GBP 1.2m/year |
| Land value uplift on adjacent housing | Hedonic effect of new amenity | L | non_cash | Within 800m radius | Hedonic estimate, capped at GBP 8m total |

## Excluded

- Pupil future earnings (already captured via WELLBY)
- Construction employment (transfer; demand displaced from elsewhere)
- VAT on construction (transfer)
- Tax receipts from teachers (transfer)
